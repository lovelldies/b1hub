Controlling individual devices is easy, too, thanks to Alexa's ability to recognize devices and respond appropriately to commands like "Alexa, turn TV off."

How to setup Amazon Alexa?

  • Download the Alexa app from Google Play/Apple App Store and sign in.
  • Plug the included power adapter into Echo and then into a power outlet. The light ring on Echo turns blue, and then orange. When the light turns orange, Echo greets you.
  • Follow the guided instructions in the app to connect Echo to a Wi-Fi network. To learn more, go to Connect Echo to Wi-Fi.
  • Tip: If your Echo doesn't connect to your Wi-Fi network, unplug and then plug the device into a power outlet to restart it. If you still have trouble, reset your Echo to its factory settings and set it up again. To learn more, go to Reset Your Amazon Echo or Echo Dot (1st Generation).
  • You can now use your Echo device. To get started, say the "wake word" and then speak naturally to Alexa. Your Echo device is set to respond to the wake word "Alexa" by default, but you can use the Alexa app to change the wake word at any time. In the app, go to Settings, select your Echo device, and then select Wake word.

Please visit Amazon for more information.

How do I have Amazon Alexa discover my devices?

  • Just say ‘Alexa, discover devices’ once your B.One account and Amazon accounts are linked. You can also discover devices in the ‘Connected Home’ section of the Alexa App Settings page. Select ‘Discover Devices’ to have Alexa find all connected and compatible devices.

Can I turn off Amazon Alexa's microphone?

  • Yes, you can turn OFF Amazon Alexa's microphone by pressing the Microphone ON/OFF button present at the top of the device.

What do I say to Alexa to control my devices that are paired with B.One Hub?

  • To turn ON the device, say “Alexa, TURN ON <device name>”.
  • To turn OFF the device, say “Alexa, TURN OFF <device name>”.
  • To set the dimming level of Dimmers, Philips Hue lights and Cree Bulb, say “Alexa, SET <device name> to <value> PERCENT”.
  • To control the temperature in Thermostat, say “Alexa, set H to <value> Degrees.

Note: <device name> refers to the actual device name in the B.One app. <value> refers to numeric figure ranges from 1 to 100. For Thermostats, it depends upon the minimum and maximum temperature of the respective Thermostat.
